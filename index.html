About Me

I'm a public health practitioner, ceramic artist, and writer based in Portland, Oregon. My work bridges the worlds of design, health equity, and community storytelling. I’m currently conducting a Fulbright research fellowship in Taipei, Taiwan, exploring how home environments shape health — and how local communities and traditions influence housing design and environmental policy.

With a background in U.S. housing policy and grassroots public health, I’m interested in how space, culture, and history intersect in everyday life. This site is a place to document research, field notes, and reflections from my time in Taiwan.
